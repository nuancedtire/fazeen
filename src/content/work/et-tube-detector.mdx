## What it was

This was a prototype Mask R-CNN system built to detect endotracheal tubes on neonatal chest X-rays and flag whether placement looked correct. It was one of the earliest clinical AI projects built as a proof of concept, before the difference between a model demo and a clinically safe workflow was fully understood.

## What we built

The project used a Mask R-CNN style pipeline to localize the tube and produce image-level detections on chest radiographs. The report also includes the supporting architecture diagrams we used to reason about the workflow, including a vision-to-network concept figure and a Region Proposal Network diagram.

## What it taught me

The main lesson was that clinical AI is not just about detection accuracy. The harder problems are consistency, workflow fit, and deciding when a model is good enough to be trusted in a high-stakes setting.

## Why it is deprecated

This project is now presented as a historical prototype rather than an active tool. It reflects an early stage in the work: useful for learning, important to show, but not something to position as production-ready clinical software.
